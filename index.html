About

Technical product management isn't a new direction for me — it's naming work I was already doing.

I spent years designing learning experiences before formally training as a software engineer, and I've spent the years since working the seam between the two: first as an Instructional Designer at McGraw Hill, then as a contributor to open-source engineering projects, now pursuing a Technical Product Manager role that makes that translation work my full-time job.

The instinct is consistent across both sides: identify the actual bottleneck behind a workflow, scope what a fix should and shouldn't do, and stay close enough to the content and the engineering to know when a solution is real versus when it just looks real.

TE-to-Course
Alignment Accelerator

Ranjani Indrajith — PM  ·  Jamila Gibson — Instructional Design

An AI-assisted workflow that extracts structured lesson-level asset, materials, and component data from Spanish-language Teacher Editions — replacing a 60-hour-per-unit manual alignment process with a verified, QC-ready spreadsheet output in under 15 hours.

Program Scale
01 Diagnosed the root cause — the CAT tracker meant to bridge TE and course build was cloned from the ELA program and didn't reflect SLA-unique components, forcing manual re-extraction with no reusable output.
02 Designed the AI-assisted workflow — prompt engineering and LLM-assisted extraction from TE PDFs into structured, spreadsheet-ready output.
03 Kept humans in the loop by design — shifted the team's role from creating extractions to reviewing AI-generated drafts, preserving accuracy under a 9-day deadline.
04 Scoped what came later — deferred a custom-built tool until usage data justified it; sequenced the CAT tracker fix after the extraction workflow, not bundled into v1.
